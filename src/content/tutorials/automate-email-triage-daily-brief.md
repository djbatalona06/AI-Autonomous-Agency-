Automating email triage means having every incoming message pre-sorted into urgent, follow-up, info, or junk, with a ready-to-edit reply drafted for the ones that actually need a response — so you open your inbox to a prioritized list instead of a wall of unread mail.

## Why triage is the highest-leverage automation for solo operators

If you're the only person handling sales, support, and admin, your inbox is effectively your task queue — but it's an unsorted one. Every minute spent scanning past newsletters and CC threads to find the one email that needs a same-day reply is a minute not spent on the work that actually moves the business.

## What the automation does

1. **Classify.** Every incoming email is automatically categorized (urgent, follow-up, info, junk) based on sender, content, and your own historical patterns.
2. **Draft.** For anything in the urgent or follow-up bucket, a reply draft is generated and left ready for a quick edit and send — not auto-sent.
3. **Brief.** A single Slack DM each morning at 7 AM summarizes today's meetings, the emails needing a reply, and the tasks due — before you've opened your inbox at all.
