Recovering abandoned carts automatically means running a 3-touch email sequence that reminds the shopper, escalates to a discount if they still haven't converted, and stops the instant they buy — with zero manual intervention. For most stores doing $5K+/month, this single automation recovers more revenue than any other change to the checkout flow.

## Why carts get abandoned in the first place

Shoppers abandon for all kinds of reasons — a distraction, sticker shock at shipping cost, or just comparison-shopping elsewhere. Most of them aren't gone forever; they need a nudge at the right moment, not a guilt-trip email six days later.

## The 3-touch sequence

1. **Touch 1 (within 1 hour):** A friendly reminder with the exact items still in cart — no discount yet, just a nudge.
2. **Touch 2 (within 24 hours):** A follow-up that addresses common objections (free shipping threshold, return policy) if they still haven't converted.
3. **Touch 3 (48–72 hours):** A time-limited discount, positioned as a last nudge rather than the default expectation.

The sequence stops automatically the moment the shopper completes checkout — no risk of an embarrassing discount email arriving after they've already paid full price.
